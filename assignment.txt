# AI Systems Engineer — Case Study

---

## Background

You are interviewing for an **AI Systems Engineer** role at Noon Academy, an EdTech company in Saudi Arabia.

Noon runs hybrid test-prep classrooms where students attend physical locations with facilitators managing each classroom, while teachers deliver instruction remotely to multiple classrooms in parallel. Students are expected to:

- Attend morning sessions (90 minutes, Mon-Thu)
- Complete evening practice at home

**The Problem:**

In a parallel universe, Boon Academy is doing the same thing as Noon. Boon is running into a problem: when students fall behind, facilitators are supposed to intervene — call parents, schedule 1-on-1 tutoring, send motivational messages, etc. However, **only ~30% of students who fail quizzes actually receive any intervention before the next quiz.** Facilitators say they're overwhelmed and don't know who to prioritize.

**Boon Academy is scaling from 1,000 students (18 campuses) to 5,000 students (100 campuses) next year. Intervention at scale will completely collapse.**

---

## Your Challenge

<aside>
➡️

Context: It's Day 14. Quiz 1 (Quantitative) happened on Day 10. Quiz 2 (Verbal) is scheduled for Day 20 — just 6 days away.

</aside>

**Fix this problem before it's too late — we already left students behind before Quiz 1, we have six days to ensure we're not repeating that mistake before Quiz 2.**

Design and build an intelligent system that dramatically improves intervention rates while keeping facilitator workload manageable.

**Success looks like:**

- Intervention rate: 30% → 80%+
- Facilitators can actually use what you build
- The right students get help at the right time
- The system improves over time as it learns

**You decide:**

- What to build (pipeline? API? dashboard? automation engine? all of the above?)
- What technology stack to use
- How AI/LLMs fit into the solution
- How it integrates into facilitator workflows
- How to prioritize which students need help most urgently

---

## Constraints

- **Timeline:** Must work for 20 campuses in 2 days, but be architected to scale to 100 campuses in 6 months
- **Budget:** $200/month for tools, APIs, subscriptions, etc.
- **Team:** You'll have 1 engineer part-time to help maintain this after you ship it
- **Users:** Facilitators are comfortable with WhatsApp and Google Sheets, but not highly technical
- **Data quality:** Real operational data is messy — missing values, inconsistencies, errors. Your system should handle this gracefully, not break.

## What We Expect

You will be judged only on what you actually submit. 
Missing any of the four deliverables below means we won't review your submission. 

### 1. GitHub repository

- Runnable from a fresh clone with a single command (`make demo`, `python main.py`, `docker compose up` — your choice)
- All paths read from environment variables. **No hardcoded paths**.
- All secrets read from environment variables. **No hardcoded API keys, even as fallbacks**.
- A `requirements.txt` or equivalent that locks dependencies.
- `README.md` at the root explaining how to run it in under 30 lines.

### 2. `analysis.md` (one page, max ~600 words)

A written memo with five sections, in this order:

1. **Diagnosis (3 sentences):** what's the real problem behind the 30% intervention rate, in your own words.
2. **What you found in the data (3 bullets):** at least three specific numbers you pulled from the dataset. Not vibes — numbers.
3. **What you built and why (5 bullets max):** the components, in order, with one-sentence reasoning per component.
4. **What you cut and why:** name two things you decided NOT to build, and why those were the right cuts.
5. **What you'd build next:** the single most important thing you'd add if we gave you one more week.

If your analysis is longer than one page, we'll only read the first page.

### 3. `outputs/` folder, committed to the repo

The actual files your system produces when you run it end-to-end *with the LLM enabled* on the provided data. We will look at this folder to verify your system runs as you describe it. Empty or stub outputs are an automatic fail.

### 4. A 5–10 minute video walkthrough (Loom or equivalent link in the README)

A screen recording of you running your own system, end-to-end, with the LLM on, on the provided data. Show:

- The system starting up
- The pipeline running to completion
- The actual outputs being produced
- A walkthrough of 2–3 specific students at different risk levels

## Live Presentation (60 minutes)

### Part A — Demo (30 minutes)

You will share your screen and:

1. Run your system live, end-to-end, with the LLM enabled.
2. Walk us through 3–4 specific students at different risk levels.
3. Show us the code where you made interesting choices (not all of it).
4. Tell us what you cut and what you'd build differently.

You drive. We will not interrupt. Plan for 30 minutes flat — we'll cut you off at minute 30 to move to Q&A.

### Part B — Q&A (30 minutes)

We'll go deep on:

- The data — what you found, what you didn't notice
- System design — your architecture choices and their failure modes
- AI usage — your prompts, fallbacks, and how you measure quality
- Scaling — what breaks at 100 campuses and what you'd re-engineer

## What I am NOT Looking For

❌ **Over-engineered systems** — We want something that works today and can evolve, not a Kubernetes cluster for 20 campuses.

❌ **AI for the sake of AI** — If a simple rule does the job, say so. We want to see judgment about when AI adds value.

❌ **No-code only** — Zapier/Airtable can be part of your stack, but we need to see real code. This is an AI engineering role.

❌ **Perfect polish** — An imperfect system that runs is better than a perfect architecture that's just a diagram.
